Connectivity.

Background

In partnership with the University of Edinburgh and the City of Edinburgh Council, the Connectivity. project is dedicated to the health and sustainability of Edinburgh's greenspaces. We offer a detailed practical guide and results for practitioners focused on targeted conservation, and an interactive webmap to test habitat connectivity scenarios. This initiative is designed to support professionals, researchers, and other urban landscape change-makers in making well-informed decisions for sustainable urban development and ecological preservation.

Our Aim

Our aim is to model habitat connectivity and environmental deprivation for targeted conservation in Edinburgh, where, biodiversity action plans, coherent with national policy, call for connectivity enhancement between fragmented habitats.

Objectives

  • 1) Model habitat connectivity for priority species
  • 2) Validate connectivity models with field-based observations
  • 3) Analyse connectivity results and field observations to model environmental deprivation
  • 4) Produce recommendations on environmental deprivation-targeted improvements to habitat connectivity
