Species Information

Eurasian Badger
Meles meles

The Eurasian badger is a protected species in the UK, including Scotland. While less threatened than red squirrels, badgers face challenges such as habitat loss and fragmentation. Conservation efforts in and around Edinburgh focus on protecting the habitats of Eurasian badgers. Its ideal habitat is a mix of woodlands with undergrowth near open fields or meadows. For the context of mapping, it has a home range of 2.62 km squared and was given a moving window radius of 900m.

Eurasian Red Squirrel
Sciurus vulgaris

The red squirrel was chosen for mapping due to its legally protected status in Britain. Once native to all of Britain, it is now rarely seen outside of Scotland. The current British population is estimated at 140,000 individuals, of which at least 75% are found in Scotland. Unfortunately, competition from the non-native grey squirrel often displaces red squirrels. In Edinburgh, conservation efforts are in place to protect and support the red squirrel population. These initiatives include habitat conservation, monitoring, and measures to control the spread of the grey squirrel.

West European Hedgehog
Erinaceus europeaus

Once a common sighting in Edinburgh, hedgehogs now face various challenges, including habitat loss, road traffic accidents, and changes in land use. It is estimated that the population has declined 50% since the 1990s, once numbering 36 million in Britain in the 1950s. Local wildlife organisations and communities actively engage in initiatives such as creating hedgehog-friendly habitats and installing hedgehog highways.
